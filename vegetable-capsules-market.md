<p><p>The vegetable capsules market is primarily categorized by the types of encapsulating agents used, including plant polysaccharides, starch, and hydroxypropyl methylcellulose (HPMC). Plant polysaccharides are natural polymers derived from plants, offering a vegan-friendly option. Starch-based capsules are biodegradable and commonly utilized for their accessibility and cost-effectiveness. HPMC capsules, made from modified cellulose, are favored for their stability and low moisture content, making them suitable for a variety of formulations. These types cater to the growing demand for clean-label and vegetarian products.</p></p>
