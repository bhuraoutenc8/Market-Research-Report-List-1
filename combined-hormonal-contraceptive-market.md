<p><p>The combined hormonal contraceptive market comprises three primary types: pills, patches, and rings. Pills are oral contraceptives that combine estrogen and progestin to prevent ovulation, commonly taken daily. Patches are transdermal systems that release hormones through the skin, offering weekly convenience. Rings are flexible devices inserted into the vagina, releasing hormones over a month, allowing for easy and discreet use. Each type provides effective birth control while catering to different preferences in administration and lifestyle.</p></p>
