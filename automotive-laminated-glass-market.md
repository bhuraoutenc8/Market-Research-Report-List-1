<p><p>The automotive laminated glass market is divided into Class A and Class B types, characterized by their application and manufacturing standards. Class A laminated glass is primarily used for front and side windows, emphasizing safety, clarity, and visibility. It features high-performance coatings and enhanced shatter resistance. In contrast, Class B laminated glass is mainly utilized for lower-tier applications like rear windows and sunroofs, focusing on cost-effectiveness and basic safety features. Both types play essential roles in improving vehicle aesthetics and occupant protection.</p></p>
